# CaaS Lite Pilot — Scope Statement

**Status:** Working document. v1.0, drafted 2026-05-22.
**Owner:** Project lead.
**Audience:** Project lead, integration team (incoming), future technical maintainers.

This document defines the scope of the first deliverable for market test: a single Lite Pilot engagement with one named SMB client, executed end-to-end over 30 days. Everything in this document is in service of getting that one pilot to "Day 30 success criteria met."

This is deliberately narrower than the full platform described in the project brief. The narrowing is the point — it makes the project finishable. Items intentionally out of scope are listed in § 6 so the deferral is explicit, not accidental.

---

## 1. What "Lite Pilot completed" means

The Lite Pilot is considered complete when **one SMB client has been taken through the 30-day shadow-governance journey, and all four Day-30 deliverables are in their hands.**

The four Day-30 deliverables, per the brief:

1. **Evidence Vault Log** — A PDF report demonstrating cryptographically traceable records of every AI decision the pilot client's systems made during the monitoring window.
2. **Executive Scorecard** — A compliance rating (e.g., "98% Compliant" for the EU AI Act) generated by the Verification Engine against the policy maps.
3. **Live Trust Badge** — An embeddable badge the client can place on their public website, linking to a read-only view of their Evidence Vault summary.
4. **Insurance Referral** *(optional in v1)* — A discount code from a partnered insurer based on the client's verified governance posture. May be deferred if insurance partnerships aren't yet in place.

If items 1–3 are real, working, and in the client's hands, the pilot is complete and the platform has demonstrated end-to-end value.

## 2. The 30-day journey, broken down

This restates the brief's roadmap with concrete deliverables and acceptance criteria per week.

### Week 1 — The Hook

**Objective:** Connect the pilot client's existing systems to the platform so the platform can observe what their AI is doing.

**Concrete work:**
- Identify the pilot client's AI systems and where their logs/metadata live (CRM, data warehouse, model API, etc.).
- Stand up one micro-connector or webhook endpoint per data source.
- Verify ingestion: data is arriving at the platform, in a recognisable shape, at a usable cadence.
- Generate an Initial Risk Scan report — a one-time snapshot of "based on what we can see, here are your visible compliance gaps."

**Acceptance criteria for Week 1:**
- At least one live data feed from the client into the platform.
- Initial Risk Scan delivered to the client (PDF or dashboard view).
- Client has seen the report and engaged with it (not just received it).

### Week 2 — Shadow Audit

**Objective:** Run the Verification Engine in listen-only mode against the live data, demonstrating real-time visibility without disrupting the client's operations.

**Concrete work:**
- Configure the Verification Engine with at least one policy map relevant to the client (likely EU AI Act-derived for v1; GDPR if data-privacy-heavy).
- Begin scoring incoming events against the policy map.
- Build (or activate) the Shadow Scan Dashboard so the client can watch in real time.

**Acceptance criteria for Week 2:**
- At least one full policy map loaded and producing scored events.
- Shadow Scan Dashboard visible to the client, showing live data.
- Real-time Drift Map — a view of how the client's AI behavior has evolved over the week — is generated and shareable.

### Week 3 — Vaulting

**Objective:** Begin writing tamper-evident records of every observed AI decision to the Evidence Vault.

**Concrete work:**
- Activate the Evidence Vault writer. Every scored event from Week 2 onward gets a hashed, timestamped record stored.
- Implement (or verify) the cryptographic signing of vault entries.
- Verify retrieval — the platform can produce, on demand, a signed audit trail covering any time window.

**Acceptance criteria for Week 3:**
- Vault is receiving and storing records continuously.
- A test retrieval (e.g., "show me all decisions from yesterday between 14:00 and 15:00") returns complete, signed, time-ordered records.
- The vault storage is genuinely tamper-evident in some defensible sense. *See § 5 for the integrity-of-vault question that needs answering before this week begins.*

### Week 4 — The Reveal

**Objective:** Hand the client the four Day-30 deliverables. Close the pilot.

**Concrete work:**
- Activate the Executive Dashboard for the client's account, showing their compliance scorecard.
- Generate the Evidence Vault Log PDF from the records collected in Weeks 1–3.
- Issue the Live Trust Badge (embeddable widget + the public-facing read-only vault view it links to).
- If insurance partnership is live: issue the insurance referral code. If not, deliver items 1–3 only and mark item 4 deferred.
- Conduct a Day-30 review meeting with the client. Capture their feedback.

**Acceptance criteria for Week 4:**
- All in-scope Day-30 deliverables in the client's hands.
- Day-30 review meeting held; written summary of client feedback stored in project notes.
- Decision made on next step with that client: convert to paid subscription, extend pilot, or close.

## 3. What the platform must do to support the pilot

Working backwards from the Week 1–4 work above, here is the minimum platform functionality the pilot requires. Anything outside this list is out of scope for the pilot, even if it's in the longer-term architecture.

**Must work for the pilot:**

- **Authentication** — pilot client's Executive can log in to their own dashboard. Operator (you) can log in to manage. (Currently working for the operator; the pilot client's account must be provisionable.)
- **Tenant isolation** — pilot client's data is scoped to their tenant and not visible to other tenants. (Schema supports this; needs verification that handlers enforce it consistently.)
- **At least one micro-connector or webhook** — to ingest the pilot client's data. The specific shape depends on the pilot client. *Must be built or adapted to fit.*
- **At least one policy map** — EU AI Act or GDPR or similar, machine-readable, loaded into the Verification Engine. *Must be built or imported.*
- **The Verification Engine** — in listen mode, scoring incoming events against the loaded policy map. *Existence and functional state to be verified — see gap map.*
- **The Evidence Vault** — capable of storing tamper-evident, signed, retrievable records. *Existence and integrity properties to be verified — see gap map.*
- **The Executive Dashboard** — showing the client their compliance scorecard, audit trail summary, and historical view. *Partially exists; gaps need scoping.*
- **The Shadow Scan Dashboard** — live view of incoming events being scored. *Status unknown — see gap map.*
- **The Trust Badge** — embeddable widget plus the public read-only vault view it links to. *Existence to be verified.*
- **PDF export** — for the Evidence Vault Log and the Initial Risk Scan. *Status unknown.*

**Must NOT break during the pilot:**

- Backups (Litestream to R2). Working. Leave it alone.
- Deployment pipeline. Working. Don't experiment during pilot weeks.
- Existing auth for the operator. Working. Don't touch.

## 4. What "one named SMB client" requires

The pilot cannot start without a real client. The brief targets "FinTech, Financial Institutions, E-commerce, Logistics Firms, Startups" using AI for decisioning (credit, recommendations, fraud detection, hiring).

**Before Week 1 of the pilot can begin, the following must be true:**

- A specific company has agreed in writing (email is fine, contract is better) to be the pilot client.
- They have named a point of contact who will participate in the four weekly milestones.
- They have identified which of their AI systems will be monitored.
- They have agreed to provide the necessary access — webhook endpoints, log exports, API credentials, whatever shape the integration takes.
- A pilot agreement covers: scope, duration, what data is collected, where it's stored, who sees it, what happens at Day 30.

**This is itself critical-path work.** The platform built for a hypothetical SMB will be built for the wrong SMB. The specific pilot client's situation defines the shape of the micro-connector, the choice of policy map, the design of the Executive Dashboard view, and the wording of the Trust Badge.

If no candidate pilot client is identified, the work to find one is the first work to do — earlier than any platform work beyond the stopgaps already listed.

## 5. Open questions that must be answered before each phase

These are the questions whose answers shape the work. They cannot all be answered by engineering alone.

**Before Week 1:**
- Who is the pilot client? (Open — see § 4.)
- What is their primary AI system, and where do its logs live?
- What policy map do they need first — EU AI Act, GDPR, sector-specific (e.g. credit-decision fairness for a fintech), or something else?
- What's their definition of success at Day 30? (May differ from the platform's; both must be met.)

**Before Week 3:**
- *What makes the Evidence Vault "tamper-evident"?* This is the question that determines whether the Trust Badge is meaningful. WORM storage? Hash-chained records? Externally-anchored timestamps? Cryptographic signing with what key, rotated how? The brief says "WORM storage" and "cryptographically signed evidence." Reality must match. This is a credibility-of-the-whole-product question, not a Week 3 implementation detail.

**Before Week 4:**
- What does the Trust Badge actually link to? A page hosted by the platform showing the client's compliance summary? A signed PDF? A live API response? The brief calls it a "Digital Certificate of Fairness" — the realisation of that phrase is a design decision.
- Is the insurance partnership live for v1, or deferred?

**Before any external claim is made about the pilot:**
- The compliance copy on the login page (HIPAA / PCI-DSS / ISO 28000 / AML/KYC) — clarified earlier — represents what the platform seeks to help clients achieve, not what the platform itself is certified for. The page wording should make this distinction unambiguous before the pilot client sees it.

## 6. Explicitly out of scope for the Lite Pilot

These come from the brief and the longer architecture vision. They are not "we will never build these." They are "they are not required for the first market test."

- **Active enforcement.** Red Arrows from the architecture remain disabled. The pilot is shadow-mode only.
- **CI/CD compliance gates.** No injection into the pilot client's deployment pipeline.
- **Kubernetes sidecars for production monitoring.** No in-line model isolation.
- **Multiple simultaneous pilots.** One client at a time until the playbook is proven.
- **The freelancer sales channel.** No Partner Portal, no payout engine, no 15% residual commission flow. (Defer to post-pilot if the first pilot succeeds and demand grows.)
- **iPaaS connectors for general SaaS tools** (Zapier, MuleSoft, broad CRM/accounting). Only the specific connector(s) required for the named pilot client.
- **Coverage of all four compliance regimes** (HIPAA, PCI-DSS, ISO 28000, AML/KYC). The pilot delivers against whatever framework the pilot client actually needs.
- **AI-BoM, Bias Monitoring tools, Smart Document Automation, Scenario Modeling, Fraud Detection** — all referenced in the brief as "Scaling" features. None are part of the Lite Pilot.
- **Auditor Portal for regulators.** Not required for SMB pilot; required later for regulated enterprise clients.
- **Self-registration as a customer acquisition channel.** Pilot clients are hand-onboarded; self-registration remains disabled until its security posture is decided.

## 7. Definition of pilot success vs. project success

**Pilot success** = the four Day-30 deliverables in one client's hands, with a documented Day-30 review.

**Project success** = pilot success + the client signs a paid subscription OR explicitly endorses the platform for a case study OR refers another client. In other words, market validation, not just delivery.

The distinction matters. Delivering the four artifacts but having the client walk away unconvinced is a partial outcome — it tells you the platform works but the value isn't compelling. Both pilot and project success should be tracked separately.
